The Parametric Furnace

There are a few scientific and economic properties of modern AI, centered around foundation models, that I used to think were unique and unprecedented.

  • Scaling laws, or the smooth and predictable improvement in model performance with economic inputs (compute)
  • A training stack where:
    • Stages/components are at once modular and integrated
    • Improvements across the stack almost always compound
  • Emergent capabilities, where continuous changes in inputs can lead to discontinuous changes in outputs
  • Self-acceleration, where better foundation models accelerate AI research itself

In fact, I will argue that heavy industry often shares some or all of these characteristics, and that we should look toward them to understand how AI will play out over the next decade. By heavy industry, I mean things like blast furnaces, offshore oil/gas rigs, and electric power plants.
