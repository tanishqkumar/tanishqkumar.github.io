What would the ancients think of us?

many believe RL on LLMs is "enough for AGI." as we change focus from pretraining to inference/RL as a community, it seems worth briefly taking stock of where we are in the grand quest to imbue metal with cognition. what would classical thinkers on computation and AI might think about deep learning and foundation models? in this spirit, i've read and reflected on a few classical AI papers from the 20th century, including those by turing, shannon, and mccarthy. Alan Turing, "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" - surprised how driven his beliefs on the possibility of AI by the aesthetics of universal computation - the telepathy/ESP bit at the end is hilarious, sort of his newton-alchemy moment Claude Shannon, “A Mathematical Theory of Communication” John McCarthy, "Programs with Common Sense"
