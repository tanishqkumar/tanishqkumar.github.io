Research

I work on the science of deep learning. Most recently I have been thinking about synthetic data. I've been fortunate to learn how to do science from Sam Gershman and Aditi Raghunathan.

I am interested in:

  • What systems centered around foundation models will look like in 5-10 years, and how they will touch our lives in unthinkable ways
  • Similarities and differences between artificial and natural learning systems
Working on the latter requires understanding all parts of the deep learning stack, from pre and post training to inference and deployment.

I think deep learning moves fast enough that anyone with strong fundamentals in mathematics and good engineering skills can contribute to the frontier. I have been lucky enough to be mentored by folks working on things ranging from foundation model pretraining to muP/feature learning theory to mouse olfaction. For this diversity of experiences and points of view I am immensely thankful.
