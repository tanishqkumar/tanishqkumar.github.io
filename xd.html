Gap Year Goals

Here I outline some of the high level goals I have for this gap year, ones that I'm on track and hope to hit. Ambitious, but realistic--just barely achievable. They are listed in descending order of priority.

  • Software Engineering: Have 5 fully-fledged web apps as projects to showcase full-stack proficiency, incorporating React, Django, and knowing the ins and outs of other frameworks, like Flask, Node, as well as devOps via Heroku. Understand all industry lingo reasonably well, from Kubernetes/Docker to MongoDB. Have 5 fully-fledged mobile apps built with React Native that encompass a range of functionalities, so that I'm confident I can prototype any industrial idea I have. Know which CS courses I'd like to take at Harvard, and why.
  • Industry Thinking: Have thought about problem spaces in 6 different industries. Have strong opinions on the viability of working as a tech entrepreneur in each industry informed by reading, sure, but also online research, as well as talking to selected experts. Have 1-2 industries, and a few problems in those industries, that I'm not only very informed about, but am also ideating solutions for. Know which problems are the most profitable, why they haven't been worked on, and otherwise, the problems most important for human progress.
  • Valley Thinking: Have notes on 50+ meetings with Bay Area-specific people. Have attended 25+ events on entrepreneurship --conferences, meetups, etc.--in the area, and got a deep, vocational knowledge of the exact steps involved in starting and scaling an early stage company, from investors and founders alike. Understand why Silicon Valley is the world's tech hub, and the reasons for moving over here to start a company. Know 5+ big-name investors at least somewhat well on a personal level.
  • World History: Have read 10 biographies on some of the most influential people in human history--from Aristotle to Einstein, da Vinci to Shakespeare-- and understood 2-3 themes to consciously take action on. Have read 5 books on world history and progress, understanding the most important historical events, innovations and inventions (and how they came about) that brought us to where we are today. Be able to synthesize insights in conversation across these topics.
  • Breakdancing: Have a beautiful toprock and groove down, where there are 10+ moves seamlessly blended together and non-repeated every time I perform, and I can perform without a problem to songs I've never heard before just based on intuition. I have 6+ footwork moves down solid, as well as their transitions looking stunning. I've mastered windmills and swipes, and incorporate those to kill the beat. I've performed several times at parties, clubs, and even bboy battles, enjoying it every time.
  • Pick-Up: Cold approach is instinctive at this point, both daygame and at social events, with 100+ cold/lukewarm approaches behind me, each, and extensively documented experience on attraction/escalation, as well as physical experience hovering around 10L. Tension is fun at this point, and non-neediness is palpable purely because of experience at this point. Mastered storytelling and studied humour in a platonic context, having implemented both consciously in several instances, with notes and iteration.
  • Writing: 20+ long pieces on web-site, anlaysed 20+ pieces of great work and implemented several changes as a result of seeing good writing. Gotten feedback on a few from those great writers, or otherwise--and iterated accordingly. Larger vocabulary, more elegant thinking, marginally less purple prose and more mature style. Compare my final works to those of the writers I'm examining until they are just as good, if not better, in blind tests with intelligent friends.
  • Miscellaneous: Learning and mastering the fundamentals of breakdance, learned the fundamentals of formal dance--tango, waltz, foxtrot, and more. Have learned how to sail, can spend times at sea alone and safely, and work productively while doing so. Have attended a 10-day meditation retreat, ruminated over world problems, and have an opinion on the power of meditation.
