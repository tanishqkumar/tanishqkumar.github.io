I'm a freshman at Harvard studying mathematics. I grew up in 8 cities, London and Abu Dhabi chief amongst them.

Currently Taking: Vector Calculus, Data Structures and Algorithms, Statistical Inference, Machine Learning, Expository Writing.

Next Semester: Groups and Rings, Dynamical Systems, Computer Graphics, Modern European History.

Currently Reading: The Private Lives of the Impressionists, The Structure of Scientific Revolutions, Skunk Works.

Culture & Childhood

I have been to school with sons of Guatemalan immigrants and Emirati oyster farmers, with the daughters of Indian cobblers and British royalty. Growing up, I moved from Kentucky to New York to Ohio, then to Delhi, Abu Dhabi, London, San Francisco, and soon, to Boston. I speak passable Hindi and French, and am trying to pick up Mandarin.

Schooling & Academia

I spent the first two years of high school at Cranleigh, Abu Dhabi, and the second two at Westminster School, London. I took A-levels in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, and loved the UK enough to consider taking up an offer to study physics at Cambridge before opting to come stateside instead.

Future Goals

Though I came into college intent on a PhD in computer science followed by a career in "deep tech", I am currently pretty enamored with the study of economics, and am considering a career in academia. In any case, I'd like to spend a few years in each of Shanghai, Paris, São Paulo and Sydney no matter how the next few decades play out.

Hobbies

When I'm not working, I'm breakdancing or playing golf. I'm currently working on my windmill. Then, I want to learn swipes, 90s, turtle spins, and handhops. I'm a scratch golfer, as living in Abu Dhabi gave plenty of sunshine in which to practise. California, where I spent much of my gap year, was an ideal place to do both things.
