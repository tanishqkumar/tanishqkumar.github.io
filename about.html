I'm a freshman at Harvard studying math. I grew up in 8 cities, London and Abu Dhabi chief amongst them, and speak French and Hindi.

Culture & Childhood

I have been to school with sons of Guatemalan immigrants and Emirati oyster farmers, with the daughters of Indian cobblers and British royalty. Growing up, I moved from Kentucky to New York to Ohio, then to Delhi, Abu Dhabi, London, San Francisco, and soon, to Boston. I speak Hindi and French, and trying to pick up Mandarin.

Schooling & Academia

I spent the first two years of high school at Cranleigh, Abu Dhabi, and the second two at Westminster School, London. I took A-levels in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, and loved the UK enough to consider taking up an offer to study physics at Cambridge before opting to come stateside instead.

Future Goals

As an undergrad, I want to go deep into complexity theory and algorithms, and spend most of my free time working on software projects in urban planning and game development. After a PhD in computer science, I hope to start deeply technical companies (like Improbable and Ayasdi), spending a few years in each of Shanghai, Paris, São Paulo and Sydney in the process.

Hobbies

When I'm not working, I'm breakdancing or playing golf. I'm currently working on my windmill. Then, I want to learn swipes, 90s, turtle spins, and handhops. I'm a scratch golfer, as living in Abu Dhabi gave plenty of sunshine in which to practise. California, where I spent much of my gap year, was an ideal place to do both things.
