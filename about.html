I'm an undergrad at Harvard studying math. I grew up 8 cities, London and Abu Dhabi chief amongst them.

Past

I have been to school with sons of Guatemalan immigrants and Emirati oyster farmers, with the daughters of Indian cobblers and British royalty. I grew up moving from Kentucky to New York to Ohio, then to Delhi, Abu Dhabi, London, San Francisco, and now, Boston. I speak Hindi and French.

In high school, I took A-levels in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. After a gap year in the Bay Area working as a software engineer, I am now finishing math and CS degrees at college.

Present

This spring, I'm exploring several research ideas in machine learning.

Future

Though I came into college intent on a career in software, I plan to go to graduate school. I'm curious what the right level of abstraction to study neural networks is: theorems, infinite width/depth limits, upper bounds, first order approximations, relations to neuroscience, parameter dynamics, pure scientific empirics without theorizing, mechanistic interpretability, or some other approach. I'm fascinated by universality of learned representations, broadly defined.
