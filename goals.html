Gap Year Goals

Here I outline some of the high level goals I have for this gap year, ones that I'm on track and hope to hit. Ambitious, but realistic--just barely achievable.

  • Software Engineering: Have 5 fully-fledged web apps as projects to showcase full-stack proficiency, incorporating React, Django, and knowing the ins and outs of other frameworks, like Flask, Node, as well as devOps via Heroku. Understand all industry lingo well, from Kubernetes/Docker to MongoDB. Have completed 200+ problems on LeetCode and have mastery over use cases and implementation of algorithms/data structures, as well as have extensive experience with systems design questions via practise interview and complex projects. Similarly, have 5 fully-fledged mobile apps, some created with React Native and some built in Swift/Java, that encompass a range of functionalities, so that I'm confident I can prototype any industrial idea I have. Know which CS courses I'd like to take at Harvard.
  • Industry Reading: Have read, analysed, taken notes on, 48 books from 6 different industries. Have strong opinions on the viability of working as a tech entrepreneur in each industry informed by reading, online research, as well as talking to selected experts. Have 1-2 industries, and a few problems in those industries, that I'm not only very informed about, but am also ideating solutions for, and am convinced they are profitable as well as world-changing.
  • Valley Thinking: Have notes on 50+ meetings with Bay Area-specific people, including >10 prominent VCs/angels. Have attended 10+ events on entrepreneurship in the area, and got a deep, vocational knowledge of the exact steps involved in starting and scaling an early stage company, from investors and founders alike. Understand why Silicon Valley is the world's tech hub, and the reasons for moving over here to start a company.
  • Breakdancing: Have a beautiful toprock and groove down, where there are 10+ moves seamlessly blended together and non-repeated every time I perform, and I can perform without a problem to songs I've never heard before just based on intuition. I have 6+ footwork moves down solid, as well as their transitions looking stunning. I've mastered windmills and swipes, and incorporate those to kill the beat. I've performed several times at parties and clubs, enjoying it every time. After Jan/Feb, focus on weight training so I end year at around 65kg, 10%.
  • BCEF: Have won a $250,000+ full ride merit-scholarship to Harvard.
  • CareerFear Award Applications: Have been featured on further international media, perhaps The Guardian or BBC, for op-eds on careers thinking, counselling, adolescence, and change-making. This coupled with further international awards such as the Global Changemakers and Diana Award, and the networks/communities that come with those.
  • Language: Have spent a month in Paris, now I speak French fluently, satisfy the Harvard language requirement, and my Hindi has been polished enough at home to honestly say I speak that fluently as well.
  • Pick-Up: Cold approach is instinctive at this point, both daygame and at social events, with 100+ approaches behind me and extensively documented experience on attraction/escalation, as well as physical experience at an IO level or beyond. Tension is fun at this point, and non-neediness is palpable purely because of experience at this point. Asymptotically approaching Bond. Mastered storytelling, and have decoded good-boy alpha and implemented changes to couple Bond with a happy personality to effectively become Hubs.
  • Writing: 10+ long pieces on web-site, anlaysed all of Yehong's work and implemented several changes as a result of seeing good writing. Gotten feedback on a few--from her, or otherwise--and iterated accordingly. Larger vocabulary, more elegant thinking, marginally less purple prose. Objectively compare my final works to hers until they are just as good, if not better, having internalised all the good writing techniques by this point, which is reflected in anything and everything I write.
