Academic Learning & Notes

When learning something new, I'll never take notes. The first time I'm exposed to new content, the emphasis is always on digesting and internalising the material; whether that's a lecture on pointers and threading (CS), special relativity (physics), or romantic motifs in Pride & Prejudice (english). On the second pass through the material, though, I'll paraphrase the contents of the lecture, YouTube video or textbook I'm learning from into a pages document. And then on the third pass, my goal is to shorten my own notes. Each pass of paraphrasing demands I "explain" the material in a new, more concise manner (see the Feynman Technique.) It also produces clean, palatable notes for my own future reference.
